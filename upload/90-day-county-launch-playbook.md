# 90-day county launch playbook

How to take one county site from launch to lead-generating in 90 days. Run this on Tyrone first. Don't replicate until this works.

---

## The principle

The website is 20% of the work. The other 80% happens after launch. This playbook is the 80%.

If you can't get Tyrone to 30+ qualified leads per month in 90 days, replicating the model 31 times won't fix it — it'll multiply the problem. Prove it once. Then scale.

---

## Pre-launch (week 0)

Before the site goes live, these must exist:

- [ ] Domain purchased and DNS configured
- [ ] Hosting set up (Vercel/Cloudflare Pages recommended)
- [ ] Backend wired (form submissions, file uploads, email notifications)
- [ ] Real phone number with someone answering it during business hours
- [ ] Real email address with auto-responder set up
- [ ] Privacy policy page live
- [ ] All site spec checklist items complete
- [ ] Google Analytics 4 installed (or Plausible if avoiding cookies)
- [ ] Google Search Console verified
- [ ] Bing Webmaster Tools verified
- [ ] Backup of the entire site stored separately

If any of these are missing, don't launch. A broken launch costs more to fix than waiting a week.

## Days 1-7: Foundation week

The goal this week is "exists in the eyes of Google and humans." Nothing fancy. Just presence.

### Google Business Profile (priority 1)

This is the single most important thing in the entire playbook. Local searches are dominated by the map pack — the three businesses that show up before regular results. Google Business Profile is how you get into the map pack.

- [ ] Create Google Business Profile for Solar Tyrone
- [ ] Verify by postcard (takes 5-14 days — start day 1)
- [ ] Business name: "Solar Tyrone"
- [ ] Category: Solar Energy Company (primary), Solar Energy Equipment Supplier (secondary)
- [ ] Service area: all of County Tyrone (don't pick a single address if you serve the whole county)
- [ ] Hours: match the website exactly
- [ ] Phone: same as website
- [ ] Website URL: solartyrone.ie (or whatever the domain is)
- [ ] Description: 750 characters, mention Tyrone, towns, services
- [ ] Upload 10+ real photos: installations, team, vans, before/after, panels
- [ ] Add services: Home Solar, Commercial Solar, Farm Solar, Battery Storage, EV Chargers, Maintenance
- [ ] Add products if relevant
- [ ] Enable messaging
- [ ] Enable booking if you have a calendar tool

### Bing Places

- [ ] Create Bing Places listing (Bing has 6% market share — small but free)
- [ ] Mirror the Google Business Profile exactly

### XML sitemap submission

- [ ] Submit sitemap.xml to Google Search Console
- [ ] Submit sitemap.xml to Bing Webmaster Tools
- [ ] Request indexing of the homepage in Search Console (forces crawl)

### Initial citations (NAP consistency)

NAP = Name, Address, Phone. Google checks if your business details are consistent across the web. Inconsistencies hurt rankings. Get listed on these in week 1, with EXACTLY the same name, phone, and address as the Google Business Profile:

- [ ] Yell.com
- [ ] Yelp UK
- [ ] Thomson Local
- [ ] Hotfrog UK
- [ ] FreeIndex UK
- [ ] Cylex UK
- [ ] Scoot
- [ ] 192.com

Use the exact same format every time. "Solar Tyrone" — not "Solar Tyrone Ltd" on one and "Solar Tyrone Solutions" on another.

### Social profiles (claim now, populate later)

- [ ] Facebook Business Page (high priority — facebook reviews appear in Google)
- [ ] Instagram business account
- [ ] LinkedIn company page
- [ ] YouTube channel (for install videos later)

Just claim the names and add basic info. Posting comes later.

## Days 8-21: Content and credibility weeks

Goal: give Google something to crawl beyond the homepage, and give humans reasons to trust you.

### Build the service sub-pages

The homepage links to six service pages. Build them now, properly. Each one should be:

- 800-1500 words of unique content (not duplicated from homepage)
- Include the county name 5-8 times naturally
- Have its own H1, meta title, meta description, canonical
- Include 2-3 testimonials specific to that service
- Have a clear CTA (bill upload or call)
- Include service-specific FAQ items with FAQ schema
- Link back to the homepage and to related services

Pages to build:
- [ ] /home-solar-panels-tyrone
- [ ] /commercial-solar-tyrone
- [ ] /agricultural-solar-tyrone
- [ ] /solar-battery-storage-tyrone
- [ ] /ev-charger-solar-tyrone
- [ ] /solar-maintenance-tyrone

Each one takes 2-4 hours of writing + building. Do one per day.

### Start the blog

You need a blog to capture long-tail search traffic and to give Google fresh content signals. Start with 4 posts in the first 21 days:

**Post 1: "Solar panel cost in Tyrone 2026: complete guide"**
- 2000+ words
- Real costs, real grants, real payback periods
- Targets: "solar panel cost tyrone", "how much do solar panels cost northern ireland"
- Include a CTA to upload a bill for personalised quote

**Post 2: "Solar grants in Northern Ireland 2026: what's actually available"**
- 1500+ words
- Government schemes, NIE incentives, SEG payments explained
- Targets: "solar grants northern ireland", "solar grants tyrone"
- This post will get linked to from other sites because it's useful

**Post 3: "Is your Tyrone home suitable for solar? 7 things we check"**
- 1200+ words
- Roof orientation, shading, age, structural condition
- Includes photos of real local examples (good and bad fits)
- Targets: "is my house suitable for solar"

**Post 4: "Case study: how a Dungannon dairy farm cut electricity costs by 73%"**
- 1500+ words
- Real installation, real numbers, real customer
- Photos throughout
- This becomes a sales tool you can send to other farmers

The blog posts should be findable from the main navigation and from a /blog index page. Each post should have proper SEO metadata, structured data (Article schema), and social sharing tags.

### First citation wave (extended)

Beyond week 1, get listed on industry-specific and local directories:

- [ ] Solar Trade Association directory
- [ ] MCS certified installer directory (you should already be on this)
- [ ] NICEIC member directory
- [ ] TrustMark directory
- [ ] Checkatrade
- [ ] TrustATrader
- [ ] MyBuilder
- [ ] Rated People
- [ ] Local chamber of commerce (Omagh, Strabane, Dungannon, Cookstown)
- [ ] Tyrone Business directory if one exists
- [ ] Nidirect business listings if applicable

## Days 22-45: Reviews and reputation weeks

Goal: get to 10+ Google reviews. This is the single biggest local SEO lever after Google Business Profile itself.

### Review collection system

- [ ] Set up an automated SMS or email that goes to every customer 2 days after install
- [ ] Message: "Hi [name], thanks for choosing Solar Tyrone. If you're happy with how it went, would you mind leaving us a quick Google review? Takes 30 seconds: [direct link]"
- [ ] Use a Google review link generator to create a one-tap link
- [ ] Print review request cards to leave with customers physically
- [ ] Train installers to ask in person at handover

### Reach out to past customers

- [ ] List every customer from the last 12 months
- [ ] Personally text/call the happiest ones
- [ ] Ask each one for a Google review and a Facebook review
- [ ] Aim for 15 reviews in week 4 from existing happy customers
- [ ] Don't offer anything in exchange for reviews — Google bans this

### Respond to every review

- [ ] Within 24 hours of any review (positive or negative)
- [ ] Thank reviewers by name
- [ ] Reference specific details from the review
- [ ] For negative reviews: acknowledge, apologise, offer to resolve offline
- [ ] Never get defensive in public

### Add reviews to the website

Once you have 10+ Google reviews:
- [ ] Add Review schema to the homepage (real reviews only)
- [ ] Add a "What customers say" section pulling from real Google reviews
- [ ] Link to the Google Business Profile so people can verify

## Days 30-60: Traffic and conversion weeks

Goal: turn the site into something that actually generates leads.

### Google Ads (start day 30)

Organic SEO takes 6+ months to mature. You need leads now. Run paid ads while SEO catches up.

Budget: £30-50 per day to start (£900-1500/month)

Campaigns to run:
- [ ] Search campaign: "solar panels tyrone" + variations
- [ ] Search campaign: "solar grants tyrone"
- [ ] Search campaign: "solar installer tyrone"
- [ ] Search campaign: "solar quote tyrone"

Setup essentials:
- [ ] Conversion tracking on form submissions and phone calls
- [ ] Call extensions enabled
- [ ] Location extensions (uses Google Business Profile)
- [ ] Negative keywords: "diy", "kit", "wholesale", "jobs", "training", "free"
- [ ] Geographic targeting: County Tyrone only, no spillover
- [ ] Ad schedule: business hours only (don't waste budget when you can't answer the phone)
- [ ] Landing page: dedicated lander, not the homepage (faster, less distraction, single CTA)

Watch the cost-per-lead. Anything under £40/lead is good for solar. Anything over £80 means the landing page or targeting is wrong.

### Response time SLA

This is the single biggest conversion lever in the entire playbook. Speed beats quality.

- [ ] Every form submission gets a human response within 60 minutes during business hours
- [ ] Every after-hours submission gets a response within the first hour of the next business day
- [ ] Every phone call missed gets a callback within 30 minutes
- [ ] Set up SMS notifications to the team for new submissions
- [ ] Build a simple dashboard or use a tool like Slack/email alerts

Studies consistently show: leads contacted within 5 minutes are 9x more likely to convert than leads contacted within an hour. Within 24 hours? Conversion drops 80%.

### Conversion tracking

- [ ] Track every form submission as a conversion in Google Analytics
- [ ] Track every phone call as a conversion (use a call tracking number if budget allows)
- [ ] Track time-to-response from submission to first human contact
- [ ] Track quote-to-close ratio
- [ ] Weekly review: how many leads, what's the cost per lead, what's the conversion rate

## Days 45-75: Authority building weeks

Goal: get other websites to link to yours. Backlinks are how Google decides who's authoritative.

### Local backlink targets

- [ ] Local newspaper: pitch a story about the business or a recent install (Tyrone Constitution, Strabane Chronicle, Mid-Ulster Mail)
- [ ] Local radio: Q97.2, Six Degrees FM — pitch yourself as an expert on energy bills
- [ ] Council website: see if they have a sustainable business directory
- [ ] University of Ulster: any solar/renewable energy programmes that might link to local installers
- [ ] Local environmental groups: Sustainable Tyrone, transition towns
- [ ] Local farmers' groups: UFU branches
- [ ] Local chambers of commerce
- [ ] Local schools: if you've done any educational installations or visits

### Industry backlinks

- [ ] Solar industry blogs and publications (write guest posts)
- [ ] MCS website member spotlight
- [ ] Manufacturer partner pages (the brands of panels and inverters you install)
- [ ] HomeOwnersAlliance type sites
- [ ] Money-saving websites (MoneySavingExpert forum mentions)

### Content that earns backlinks

The case study post from week 3 should now be ready to pitch. Email local journalists with it as a story. Offer to be quoted in any solar/energy stories they write in future.

Build one piece of "linkable asset" content:
- [ ] "Northern Ireland solar map 2026" — interactive map showing solar potential by region
- [ ] OR: "Tyrone energy report 2026" — analysis of electricity costs in the county
- [ ] OR: "Free Tyrone solar calculator" — interactive tool

These get shared, cited, and linked to. They're worth more than 100 directory citations.

## Days 60-90: Optimisation and scale prep weeks

Goal: figure out what's working, double down on it, prove the model is repeatable.

### Audit what's working

Run this analysis at day 60 and day 90:

- [ ] Which pages get the most organic traffic?
- [ ] Which keywords are bringing visitors?
- [ ] Which sources of leads convert best (organic, paid, GBP, direct)?
- [ ] What's the cost per qualified lead?
- [ ] What's the lead-to-survey rate?
- [ ] What's the survey-to-quote rate?
- [ ] What's the quote-to-close rate?
- [ ] Average deal size?
- [ ] Customer acquisition cost (total spend / customers won)?
- [ ] Lifetime value (deal size + maintenance + referrals)?

If CAC is less than 25% of LTV, the model works. Scale.
If CAC is 25-50% of LTV, the model needs optimisation. Iterate before scaling.
If CAC is over 50% of LTV, the model is broken. Stop and diagnose.

### Optimisation experiments

Pick the lowest performer from your analysis and test fixes:

- If form submission rate is low: test form field count, button copy, form position
- If bounce rate is high: test hero copy, page speed, mobile experience
- If GBP isn't generating calls: test photos, posts, response time to messages
- If ads are expensive: test ad copy, landing page, audience targeting
- If reviews aren't coming in: test review request timing, message wording, follow-up

One experiment at a time. Run for 2 weeks minimum. Measure properly.

### Build the scaling system

If Tyrone is working at day 90, you're ready to replicate. Before you do:

- [ ] Document everything that worked in Tyrone
- [ ] Document everything that didn't work
- [ ] Build a county-launch checklist (this playbook becomes the template)
- [ ] Set up a project management system to track 31 county launches
- [ ] Decide who's responsible for what across 32 counties
- [ ] Build templates for: blog posts, ads, GBP descriptions, review requests
- [ ] Calculate the budget for replicating across all 32 counties
- [ ] Prioritise: which counties launch next based on population, competition, market gap

## Weekly rhythm (every week, all 90 days)

These tasks run every single week without exception:

- [ ] Monday: review last week's leads, conversions, ad spend
- [ ] Monday: respond to any reviews from the weekend
- [ ] Tuesday: post on Google Business Profile (1 post per week minimum)
- [ ] Wednesday: post 1 blog article OR 1 social post
- [ ] Thursday: send review requests to last week's installs
- [ ] Friday: weekly metrics review, plan next week's content
- [ ] Daily: respond to every form submission within 60 minutes during business hours
- [ ] Daily: respond to every Google Business Profile message within 2 hours

---

## Success metrics at day 90

You're successful in Tyrone if:

- [ ] 30+ qualified leads in month 3 (a qualified lead = bill uploaded OR phone call lasting >2 minutes)
- [ ] 10+ Google reviews on the Business Profile (4.5 stars or higher average)
- [ ] Top 3 in Google Maps for "solar panels tyrone" and "solar installers tyrone"
- [ ] First page of Google for at least 5 keyword variations
- [ ] Cost per qualified lead under £40
- [ ] Lead response time under 60 minutes consistently
- [ ] Lead-to-survey conversion rate above 40%
- [ ] At least 3 customers won that came from the website (not referrals)
- [ ] Backlinks from at least 5 local sources

If you hit these numbers, the model works. Replicate.

If you don't, fix the gap before you replicate. Replicating a broken model 31 times means 32 broken systems.

## What this playbook costs

Realistic budget for 90 days in one county:

- Domain + hosting: £50
- Stock photos or local photographer: £300-500
- Google Ads: £2,700-4,500 (£30-50/day for 90 days)
- Content writing (if outsourced): £400-800 for blog posts
- Tools (Search Console, GA, GBP, reviews tool): £0-100
- Citation submission service (optional): £100
- Misc tools and time: £200

**Total cash budget: £3,750-6,250 per county for 90 days**

Plus your time or someone's time managing it: 5-10 hours per week minimum.

For 32 counties: £120,000-200,000 over 90 days each. This is why you prove it in one county first. If it doesn't work, you've spent £6k. If you scale a broken model, you've spent £200k.

## What can go wrong

Things that have killed campaigns like this:

**Slow response times.** You launch, leads come in, nobody answers fast enough, leads go cold, conversion craters, you blame the website.

**Treating GBP as set-and-forget.** Google rewards active profiles. If you don't post, photo, and respond weekly, the algorithm deprioritises you.

**Cheap content.** AI-generated blog posts that read like AI-generated blog posts will hurt you, not help. Either write properly or don't write.

**Paid traffic without conversion tracking.** You'll spend money and have no idea what's working.

**Ignoring negative reviews.** One unanswered 1-star review costs you 30 potential customers who read it.

**Trying to scale before proving.** This is the playbook's whole point. Tyrone first. Numbers second. Replication third.

## After day 90

If Tyrone is hitting the success metrics:

- Month 4-6: launch counties 2-5 (use Tyrone as the template)
- Month 7-9: launch counties 6-15
- Month 10-12: launch counties 16-32
- Year 2: optimisation, scale paid spend, double down on highest performers

If Tyrone isn't hitting the metrics at day 90:

- Don't launch any new counties yet
- Diagnose what's broken
- Fix the single biggest issue
- Run for another 30 days
- Re-evaluate

Don't fall in love with the rollout plan. Fall in love with the numbers. The numbers tell you when to scale.
